1 # Block B description
2 Note: Block B, also referred as final block (FB), and class in the MadWeight world.
3 Description inspired by MadWeight.
4 
5 This block corresponds to an initial state with Bjorken fractions \f$q_1\f$ and \f$q_2\f$,
6 and a final state with a visible particle with momenta \f$p_2\f$, and an invisible particle
7 with momenta \f$p_1\f$, both coming from a resonance with mass \f$s_{12}\f$. Extra radiation (ISR) it is also allowed.
8 
9 The goal of this Block is to address the change of variables needed to pass from the standard phase-space
10 parametrization to the \f$\frac{1}{4\pi E_1} ds_{12} \times J\f$ parametrization. Per integration point
11 in CUBA, this Block outputs the value of the jacobian, J, and the four momenta of the invisible particle,
12 \f$p_1\f$. The system of equations needed to compute \f$p_1\f$ is described below.
13 
14 
15 ### Change of variables
16 
17 From the standard phase-space parametrisation:
18 
19 \f[
20  dq_1 dq_2\frac{d^3 p_1}{(2\pi)^3 2E_1}(2\pi)^4 \delta^4 (P_{in}-P_{out})
21 \f]
22 
23 where \f$P_{in} and P_{out}\f$ are the total four-momenta in the initial and final states, to the
24 following parametrisation:
25 
26 \f[
27  \frac{1}{4\pi E_1} ds_{12} \times J
28 \f]
29 
30 where the jacobian, J, is given by:
31 
32 \f[
33  J = \frac{E_1}{s} \left| p_{2z} E_1 - E_2 p_{1z} \right|^{-1}
34 \f]
35 
36 ### Parameters
37 
38 - Collision energy.
39 
40 ### Inputs
41 
42 - Visible particle, with 4-momentum \f$p_2\f$
43 - The invariant \f$s_{12}\f$ as output from the Flatter or NarrowWidthApproximation module.
44 
45 ### Outputs
46 
47 - Invisible particle, with 4-momentum \f$p_1\f$ (up to two solutions possible)
48 - Jacobian, one per solution.

50 ### System of equations to compute \f$p_1\f$
51 
52 The integrator throws random points in the invariant (\f$s_{12}\f$) while \f$p_2\f$ is a known quantity. The equations to
53 compute \f$p_1\f$ are:
54 
55 \f{eqnarray}{
56  s_{12} &=& (p_1 + p_2)^2 = M_{1}^{2} + M_{2}^2 + 2 E_1 E_2 + 2 p_{1x}p_{2x} + 2p_{1y}p_{2y} + p_{1z}p_{2z} \\
57  p_{1x} &=& - p_{Tx} \\
58  p_{1y} &=& - p_{Ty} \\
59  p_1^2 &=& 0 \Leftrightarrow E_{1}^2 = p_{1x}^2 + p_{1y}^2 + p_{1z}^2
60 \f}
61 
62 Where \f$\vec{p}_T\f$ is the total transverse momentum of the visible particles. Using the values of \f$p_{1x}, p_{1y}\f$ from equations (2) and (3), equation (1) can be written as \f$p_{1z} = A - B E_1\f$, where A and B are:
63 \f{eqnarray}{
64  A &=& \frac{s_{12} - M_{2}^2 + 2(p_{Tx}p_{2x} + p_{Ty}p_{2y})}{2 p_{2z}} \\
65  B &=& \frac{E_2}{p_{2z}} \\
66 \f}
67 
68 Finally equation (4) can be written as \f$(1 - B) E_{1}^2 + 2AB E_1 - C = 0\f$, where \f$C = p_{Tx}^{2} + p_{Ty}^{2}\f$.
69 
70 Each solution of the quadratic equation with a positive value of \f$E_1\f$ is taken.
